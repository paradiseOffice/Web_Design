Kirk Hallam Lake

Idyllic fishing spot in the heart of the midlands

Built as part of the Nutbrook canal in the nineteenth century, the lake offers an ideal place to relax. There are accessible paths round the lake, and over the bridge to the meadows area. A picnic area with a wheelchair friendly spot and barbeque is also available. The lake is nearby the Nutbrook cycle trail and the other lake on Straw's Bridge.

The Friends of the lake help out twice a week cutting back trees and doing other gardening tasks. Many birds are resident, including a family of swans, a heron, various ducks and Eygptian geese. Over in the meadows area there are many mature trees, flowers that butterflies love and newly planted trees.
